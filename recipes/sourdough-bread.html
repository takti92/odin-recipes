Sourdough Bread

Description

If industrial yeast wasn't around hundreds of years ago: What was the juice causing breads to rise? It's what we call Sourdough Starter now. This is the basis for most delicious and natural bread, with minimal requirements in terms of ingredients.

This recipe aims to teach you how to bake simple yet tasty Sourdough Bread.

Ingredients

  • Whole wheat flour: 200g
  • Bread flour: 800g
  • Water: 700ml
  • Sourdough Starter: 200g
  • Salt: 20g

Steps

  1. Mix flour and water and let rest for 1 hour (autolyse).
  2. Add salt and Sourdough Starter to the dough. Mix until both are fully incorporated. Then let rest for 15 minutes.
  3. Properly knead the dough for 2-3 minutes. Let rest for 15 minutes.
  4. Do Strech-and-folds and let dough rest for another 15 minutes.
  5. Do Lamination and then transfer the dough into a container suitable for bulk fermentation.
  6. Let ferment until dough has increased in size by about 70%. Do a coil-fold once in a while, if the dough has flattened out.
  7. Prepare a proofing basket and give the dough it's final shape before transferring it to the basket (also called banneton).
  8. Proof for about 2 hours (do the finger-poke test).
  9. Preheat the oven at 230ËšC with dutch oven inside.
  10. Bake the bread in the dutch oven for 35 minutes with lid and another 15 minutes without lid.
